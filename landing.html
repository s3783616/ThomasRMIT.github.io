Who am I?

My name is Thomas Jukes, Student number S3783616. My email is s3783616@student.rmit.edu.au. I was born in Australia, but I'm a dual citizen, Britain being where I grew up. I graduated secondary school at Wesley College (SKR). I'm a huge fan of Tabletop Roleplaying Games, own a cat named Lilly; and eventually want to take over the world.

Interest in IT

I've always been interested in IT. It's such a huge part of our lives, and many people, businesses and even governments depend on it. And it's growing. It's an ever expanding field; and it will become more and more impressive as technology keeps developing. I'm personally interested in delving into the world of cybersecurity, protecting various clients from intrusion. I'm inexperienced, but I'm eager to learn. And that's exactly why I went into RMIT. I hope to learn about the basics of computers, how to code, and eventually, how to get into cybersecurity.

The Ideal Job

My ideal job is either a security consultant for a corporation, a freelance pentester, or a spy. ASIS has a job called an "Operations Technology Officer". In this job, one applies their technical skills and expertise to help Australian Intelligence in their operations. The reason it appeals to me is because I can help people, (and also be a spy. I mean, who doesn't want to be a spy?)

Given the nature of the work, I'll need to be flexible, able to innovate; and *very* driven. I'll need to be able to work with people; developing superior interpersonal and liasoning skills. I need to develop my charisma, being able to work effectively with people from other countries and cultures. I've got to get good risk assessment skills, and manage myself maturely. Not to mention, a 3 year university degree.

I've got a long way to go. I can work with people pretty easily, but perhaps not to the level that ASIS requires. I plan to learn all I can through RMIT, be it cybersecurity, working with others through course projects and the like; and growing as a person.

My Personality Profile

My Myers-Briggs Test gives me the result of an ENTJ-A, or 'Commander'. It gives me a good indication as to my personality type. Working in a team is a specialty of ENTJs, but knowing the results in and of itself won't influence my behaviour. I won't need to take my results into account when working in a team, beyond predicting what situations I'll be comfortable in.

As for the online learning styles test, the results indicate that I'm a visual learner. This means little to me, I must admit. It doesn't really effect my behaviour in a team, and I don't think should be taken into account.

This means little to me, but it's interesting to see how much of me is manipulative. But hey, that's a good thing in the corporate world, right?
